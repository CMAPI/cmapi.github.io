  • Process
  • The scope of this technical committee is limited to creating a technical specification of the channels, messages, behaviors and other technical details of what is needed to enable interoperability via interwidget communications between map widgets and all other widgets. Additionally, the scope is limited to OWF based widgets.

    The criteria for success for this technical committee includes:
    1. Agreement via formal voting that the specification is sufficiently complete, with at least 75% of the technical committee voting for approval
    2. At least two implementations of the specification showing interoperability
    3. A submission of the JSON Schema for new channels, or updated JSON schema for changes to an existing channel

    Meeting Mechanisms and Frequency

    The technical committee will meet weekly, with a face to face meeting once a month. Time and Day of weekly meetings will be TBD via dial-in at TBD. Time, day and location of Face to Face meetings will be determined no later than one week prior to each meeting, and will be determined via quorum at the dial-in meeting prior to the face to face.

    Communication Mechanisms

    Outside of the regular meetings, updates, meeting notices, and regular collaboration are expected to occur via email at common-map-widget-api@googlegroups.com. This allows communications to be open, but also recorded for those who might join the group later.

    Expected Time Commitment from Members

    Weekly dial-ins are expected to be no more that two hours in length. Face to face meetings are expected to be no more than 5 hours to allow time for travel to and from the meetings. For those who volunteer to write a given section of the specification, the deadline for their section will be determined when assigned, and they will be expected to spend the appropriate amount of time to deliver their section. In no case, however, shall anyone be expected to work on this specification effort more than 25% of their time during the duration of the specification.

    Voting and Participation Rules

    In order to be able to vote on the specification, a member of the technical committee must attend at least 50% of the meetings, and actively participate in the email collaboration. Note that voting on the specification is voting only on the technical details of the specification itself, and NOT on whether any given program is bound to implement the specification.

    Anticipated Audience

    This specification is targeted for OWF widget developers and developers of libraries to be re-used by OWF widget developers.
